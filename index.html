Homefront Priorities

A community-driven, grassroots effort to hold Congressman Derrick Van Orden accountable in WI-03.

1 Campaign Overview and Our Goal

The Homefront Priorities project is a grassroots effort designed to hold Congressman Derrick Van Orden accountable within Wisconsin’s 3rd District. Our goal is to build an inclusive coalition and turn our online conversations into real-world community action. This shift is essential to connect with swing voters, working families, and veterans who feel left behind by a legislative focus on $200 billion in foreign war spending while local economic stability erodes.

Our Core Message

"Wisconsin Families First. Not Foreign Wars."

This message guides our community outreach, helping us connect local issues like gas prices and VA care back to legislative choices.

Key Audiences and Geographies

Audience Focus Primary Geographic Focus Key Local Concerns

Veterans District-wide (WI-03) VA care funding vs. $200B foreign war spending.

This guide outlines a four-step collaborative plan. Steps 1 through 3 focus on building awareness and laying the groundwork before we transition to direct community engagement in Step 4.

2 Step 1: Building Our Community Hubs

To successfully grow this movement, we need to create a welcoming and organized space for our neighbors to plug in. We are running a grassroots, community-powered effort—which means our greatest asset is people. We’ll get our shared spaces set up so everyone has what they need to contribute.

Our Team Organization

We want to make volunteering as easy as possible. We’ll use free, familiar tools to stay connected:

  • The Community Forum: We’ll set up a friendly space (like a private Facebook Group or Slack channel) where volunteers can chat, share ideas, and find activities.
  • The Shared Toolkit: All of our graphics, scripts, and resources will be kept in a shared Google Drive so anyone can easily find what they need.

Volunteer Outreach

When talking to neighbors, focus on shared economic concerns (like gas prices and healthcare) to find common ground.

  • Reach Out: Share our "Are You Feeling This Too?" message with friends, family, and neighbors on platforms like Nextdoor.
  • Join the Conversation: Post our welcoming introduction in local WI-03 community groups to invite others to help out.

3 Step 2: Preparing Our Resources

We aim to provide clear, factual context by using visual "receipts"—data points that connect national spending to our local needs. All materials are stored in our shared Digital Toolkit.

Audio, Video, and Written Resources

  • News Audio: Share factual news clips regarding the childcare funding debate to highlight domestic needs.
  • Video Templates: Organizers can record "Green Screen/Receipt" sample videos to help fellow volunteers participate in the "Make it Make Sense" conversation online.
  • Written Materials: Access pre-written templates for Facebook boards and Letters to the Editor (LTEs) tailored for the La Crosse Tribune and Leader-Telegram.

4 Step 3: Grassroots Sharing

Grassroots sharing involves bringing our message into everyday community spaces online, connecting with voters where they are already having conversations.

Activity A: Community Board Engagement

Participate in local "Buy/Sell/Trade" boards. Share our "Gas Price" and "Childcare" posts paired with the appropriate graphics to spark conversations about how legislative choices impact our daily budgets.

Activity B: Short-Form Video Push

Join the "Make it Make Sense" conversation on Reels/TikTok. Helping the Algorithm: We encourage volunteers to use local tags to reach our neighbors: #WI03 #EauClaire #LaCrosse #WisconsinPolitics.

Activity C: LTE Push

Engage with local print media. Our goal is a steady, respectful presence of 2-3 published community letters per week across the district.

5 Step 4: Public Engagement

Online momentum must now be converted into real-world conversations. We will attend public appearances to ask important questions and foster accountability. A shared calendar of local public events will help us coordinate attendance.

Public Event Q&A Guide

We encourage volunteers to partner up for public events:

  • 1. The Speaker: Focuses on asking a clear, respectful question from our guide, such as: "Why prioritize a $200B war while funding for WI-03 VA care is at risk?"
  • 2. The Videographer: Records the exchange so we can share the conversation with the rest of the community. Vertical video works best.

6 Growing the Message

To ensure our neighbors hear the answers to these important questions, we must share event footage promptly. This creates an ongoing community conversation.

Review & Edit

Highlight key responses. Add clear captions for accessibility.

Share Locally

Tag local journalists and community leaders online.

Discuss

Public Event Q&A Guide (6 Questions)

Guidance: Partner up for public events. One person records video, the other asks the question. Be respectful and constructive. Let the response speak for itself.

1. Gas & Living Costs

"Congressman, gas and groceries are increasingly expensive. How does sending $200 billion to support a war in Iran help families afford to live in Wisconsin?"

2. Veteran Support

"As a veteran yourself, how do you balance supporting $200 billion for a new war, while backing cuts to VA healthcare for the veterans living right here in your district?"

3. Childcare Trade-Off

"The administration recently said we can't afford childcare funding because of the costs of the war in Iran. Do you agree with this choice of priorities?"

4. Grocery Inflation

"Economists note the conflict is contributing to inflation. Why vote to spend $200 billion to escalate a war that is impacting our food prices?"

5. Rural Hospitals

"Rural hospitals are struggling to maintain services. Why is Congress able to find $200 billion for overseas conflicts, but struggles to support our local ERs?"

6. "America First"

"You campaigned on focusing on America's needs and limiting foreign conflicts. Since you are now supporting $200 billion for a war in Iran, do you feel this aligns with your promises?"
